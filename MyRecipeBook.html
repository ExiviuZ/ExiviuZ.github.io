Recipe #1 Adobo

  • Time: 40 minutes
  • Serves: 4 people
Ingredients Quantity
Chicken 2 lbs
Dried Bay Leaves 3 pieces
Soysauce 8 Tablespoons
Cloves of Garlic 5 pieces
Cup of Water 1 1/2
Cooking Oil 3 Tablespoons
Sugar 1 Teaspoon
Salt 1/4 Teaspoon
Whole Peppercorn 1 Teaspoon

Instructions

  1. Combine chicken, soy sauce, and garlic in a large bowl. Mix well. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour. Note: the longer the time, the better
  2. Heat a cooking pot. Pour cooking oil.
  3. When the oil is hot enough, pan-fry the marinated chicken for 2 minutes per side.
  4. Pour-in the remaining marinade, including garlic. Add water. Bring to a boil
  5. Add dried bay leaves and whole peppercorn. Simmer for 30 minutes or until the chicken gets tender
  6. Add vinegar. Stir and cook for 10 minutes.
  7. Put-in the sugar, and salt. Stir and turn the heat off. Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!

Recipe #2 Tinola

  • Time: 1 hour 5 minutes
  • Serves: 5 people
Ingredients Quantity
Chicken 2 lbs
Malunggay Leaves 1 Cup
Hot Pepper Leaves 1 Cup
Bround Black Pepper 1/8 Teaspoon
Unripe Papaya (Wedged) 1 Piece
Water 6 Cups
Knorr Chicken Cube 1 Piece
Onion (Sliced) 1 piece
Cloves Garlic (Crushed and Chopped) 1 Teaspoon
3 Thumbs Ginger (Julienne) 1 Teaspoon
Fish Sauce 2 Tablespoons
Vegetable Oil 3 Tablespoons

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot.
  2. Saut√© garlic, onion, and ginger. Add the ground black pepper.
  3. When the onion starts to get soft, add the chicken. Cook for 5 minutes or until it turns light brown.
  4. Pour the water. Let boil. Cover and then set the heat to low. Boil for 40 minutes.
  5. Scoop and discard the scums and oil on the soup.
  6. Add the Knorr chicken cube and chayote or papaya. Stir. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Put the malunggay and hot pepper leaves in the pot and pour the fish sauce in. Continue to cook for 2 minutes.
  8. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.
  9. Share and enjoy!

Recipe #3 Bistek

  • Time: 1 hour 40 minutes
  • Serves: 5 people
Ingredients Quantity
Beef Sirloin (Thinly Sliced) 1 1/2 lbs
Soy Sauce 5 Cup
Calamansi 4 Pieces
Bround Black Pepper 1/2 Teaspoon
Cloves Garlic (Minced) 3 Pieces
Yellow Onion (Sliced into Rings 3 Pieces
Cooking Oil 4 Tablespoons
Water 1 Cup
Salt 1 Pinch

Instructions

  1. Marinate beef in soy sauce, lemon (or calamansi), and ground black pepper for at least 1 hour. Note: marinate overnight for best result
  2. Heat the cooking oil in a pan then pan-fry half of the onions until the texture becomes soft. Set aside
  3. Drain the marinade from the beef. Set it aside. Pan-fry the beef on the same pan where the onions were fried for 1 minute per side. Remove from the pan. Set aside
  4. Add more oil if needed. Saute garlic and remaining raw onions until onion softens.
  5. Pour the remaining marinade and water. Bring to a boil.
  6. Add beef. Cover the pan and simmer until meat is tender. Note: Add water as needed.
  7. Season with ground black pepper and salt as needed. Top with pan-fried onions.
  8. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!
